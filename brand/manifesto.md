## Manifesto

> An illuminated drop cap sits on the first word, "For".

For five hundred years we were told to specialize. Pick a lane. Learn the thing
that pays. Somewhere in that trade we lost the Renaissance mind, the one that saw
no wall between physics and philosophy, between art and engineering, between the
head and the heart.

phy6 exists to bring it back.

Every hard idea in the universe is built from a handful of simple, beautiful
first principles. Learn those, and nothing is off-limits to you. A motor and a
moral dilemma run on the same logic. A falling apple and a falling empire rhyme.
We'll show you both.

We're not here to hand you a certificate. We want to make you formidable:
curious, hard to fool, fluent across the whole map of human knowledge. Because in
an age where AI can answer anything, the rare thing is a human who can connect
everything.

So learn for the love of it. And stop calling it wasted time. You were never
wasting time. You were becoming.

## Signature lines

Three lines are used on the page (Cormorant italic, centered, parted by a
filigree divider). The chosen trio avoids duplicating phrasing already in the
manifesto:

1. Specialization built the modern world. Connection will build the next one.
2. Start with physics. End everywhere.
3. A motor and a heartbreak run on the same logic. We'll show you.

Unused candidate (deliberately omitted — it echoes the manifesto's closing
"You were becoming."):

- You weren't wasting time. You were becoming.
